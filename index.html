Stability vs. Independence

Which would you rather have?

A lot of creative freedom and independence, but low job security

A very stable job, without much creative freedom or independence

+5 Points

Designers with more creative freedom and independence but little job security were 5% happier than designers with the opposite situation. It seems designers don't mind a bit of uncertainty as long as they can still flex their creative muscles.

–5 Points

Designers with more creative freedom and independence but little job security were 5% happier than designers with the opposite situation. It seems designers don't mind a bit of uncertainty as long as they can still flex their creative muscles.

Commute vs. Work Hours

Which would you rather do?

Commute half an hour to work, and work a normal 40 hour work week

Work from home with no commute for 50+ hours per week

+8 Points

Designers with normal hours and a long commute are 8% happier than designers with no commute and long hours. Working extra long hours might not always be worth getting to work in your pajamas.

–8 Points

Designers with long hours and no commute are 8% less happy than designers with a long commute and regular hours. Working extra long hours might not always be worth getting to work in your pajamas.

Rank vs. Responsibility

Which would you rather have?

A high-level position and 100+ employees under your management

A mid-level position with no one to manage

-6 Points

Designers with high level positions with a big team to manage are 6% happier than designers with mid-level positions and no one to manage. It seems designers are happier when they can spend more time designing and less time managing.

+6 Points

Designers with mid-level positions and no one to manage 6% happier than high-level designers with a big team to manage. It seems designers are happier when they can spend more time designing and less time managing.

Salary vs. Value

Which would you rather do?

Make a $100k+ salary, but not feel valued at your workplace

Make a $50k salary or less, but feel highly valued at work

-20 Points

Designers who are payed a lot but are valued little are 20% less happy than designers in the opposite situation. Looks like money can’t always buy happiness!

+20 Points

Designers who are payed little but are highly valued are 20% happier than designers in the opposite situation. Looks like money can’t always buy happiness!

The Value of Feeling Valued

Of all the contributing factors to happiness, whether or not a designer feels valued at their organization makes the most dramatic difference. Even when offset by a thing that many people aim for in their careers, a high salary, it’s still much better to feel valued.

It’s impossible to get a positive score if you pick money over feeling valued!
